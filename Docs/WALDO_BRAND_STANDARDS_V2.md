## A note before you begin

This document exists because Waldo is worth protecting.

Not because rules are important — but because the thing we've built is genuinely different from everything else in this space, and it will only stay different if every person who touches the brand understands what makes it that way.

Waldo is not an app. It's not a health tracker. It's not another AI product wearing a friendly coat. Waldo is the first product that treats your health as something to be handled on your behalf — quietly, competently, without you having to think about it. That idea is precise. And precision is fragile.

These standards are the tool that keeps it intact.

Read them once to understand. Return to them whenever you're unsure. When in doubt, ask yourself one question: does this feel like Waldo already handled it?

If the answer is yes, you're probably right.

# 01 · Brand foundation

## What Waldo is

Waldo is a personal AI agent that reads body signals from wearables and acts on them before the user notices they need help. It reschedules meetings, reprioritises tasks, and intervenes based on HRV, sleep, and circadian patterns — without being asked.

This is not a health tracking app. The user never opens a dashboard to manage their health. Waldo handles it.

## The core idea

Every other health product talks to the version of you that's trying. Waldo talks to the version of you that's tired of trying.

The entire product rests on one insight: you shouldn't have to manage your health. Your health should manage you. Waldo is the first brand to say that out loud — without guilt, without a wellness lecture, without a graphic of a person doing yoga at sunrise.

## The brand promise

**"Already on it."**

These three words contain the entire brand. Waldo doesn't wait. Waldo doesn't ask. Waldo has already done it. The tagline is not a marketing line — it is a description of what the product does. Protect it accordingly.

## Two words the world uses to describe Waldo

Capable and warm. Not clinical. Not cute. Not techy. The dog that already moved your slippers before you knew you needed them.

# 02 · The name

**Waldo.** Always Waldo. Never "Waldo AI." Never "Meet Waldo." Never "WALDO."

The name is a gift — it carries warmth, personality, and one of the most famous visual games in culture. "Where's Waldo?" is a free brand impression every time someone says it. The answer is always the same: already three steps ahead of you.

## Name usage rules

In product copy and UI: Waldo. Title case as a proper name. Never all caps.

In casual or referential contexts: lowercase is acceptable. "waldo sorted your Tuesday" reads correctly for social or informal marketing.

Domain: heywaldo.ai — the "hey" is part of the URL only. Do not use "hey" as a prefix in copy.

## What not to call it

- A health tracker
- An AI assistant
- A wellness app
- Waldo AI
- The Waldo app

# 03 · The mark

## The symbol

The paw print mark — four irregular ovals forming a loose cluster — is the primary symbol. Derived from the Dalmatian's spot language. The spots are not perfect circles. They are slightly irregular ovals at varying sizes. This irregularity is intentional.

## The lockup

The horizontal stack is the primary lockup: paw print symbol to the left, Waldo wordmark to the right. This is the mark used in navigation, documentation, and all formal brand contexts.

**Three colour variants:**

| Variant | Use |
|---|---|
| Black paw + black wordmark on `#FAFAF8` | Default — all light contexts |
| Orange paw + black wordmark on `#FAFAF8` | Primary marketing moments, hero sections |
| White paw + white wordmark on `#1A1A1A` | Dark contexts, reversed applications |

The circle lockup (paw print inside a filled circle) is used for app icon and favicon only.

## Clear space

Minimum clear space equals the height of the capital W on all sides of the full lockup.

## What is never done to the mark

- Stretched, compressed, or rotated
- Redrawn or approximated
- Combined with another logo or wordmark
- Given a drop shadow or visual effect
- Used in a colour not specified in these standards
- Placed on a busy photographic background

# 04 · The mascot

## Character

The Waldo mascot is a Dalmatian. Smart but goofy. Knows more than it lets on. Always acting, never posing. Never surprised. Never distressed. Never confused. Waldo has already handled whatever is happening.

## Expression

Head tilted slightly. One ear raised. Eyes with the unmistakeable look of: "I already knew that."

## The spots

Each Dalmatian spot is slightly different in size. Some form loose constellations. They are data points. The spot language connects the mascot to the product's core idea — The Spot, The Constellation, the paw print symbol. They are all the same idea in different forms.

## Style

Flat illustration. Bold outlines with natural wobble — not geometric precision. The line has chalk-like texture. Hand-drawn warmth, not mechanical construction.

**Illustration palette:**
- Primary line / fill: Waldo Black `#1A1A1A`
- Accent: Waldo Orange `#F97316` — bow tie, one accent spot. Once per illustration.
- Background: Waldo White `#FAFAF8`

## The bow tie

The bow tie is the mascot's primary accessory in every context. It bridges the playful Dalmatian and the Alfred register — warm wit with quiet competence.

## The ASCII mark

For terminal, monospace, and developer contexts. Character limit is not a constraint — the mark should read as the mascot, not as a minimal glyph.

```
  / \
 (o o)
  |▲|     ← bow tie node
 /   \
```

Or in inline/condensed form using Unicode:

```
≽^• ᴥ •^≼<{=}>
```

Where `<{=}>` represents the bow tie. The mark should be recognisable as a dog face with a bow tie at a glance, not decoded.

**Eye state variants for inline use:**

| Glyph | State |
|---|---|
| `(^• ᴥ •^)` | Watching — attentive, default |
| `(°• ᴥ •°)` | Flagging — something noticed |
| `(^‿^)` | Good — positive, peak state |
| `(-• ᴥ •-)` | Quiet — nothing to report, all clear |

Used as CLI prompt prefix, splash screen, loading state indicator.

## Rules for mascot use

The mascot is always doing something. Never static and posed. Never given a speech bubble. Never depicted as sad or frustrated. Never in a generic tech context (no circuits, no robots).

The mascot is not the speaking agent. Copy is Waldo's voice. Illustration is Waldo's presence. Never conflate the two.

# 08 · Typography

## The typefaces

### Corben — headlines only

Google Fonts. Rounded serif. Warm and slightly unexpected for the health-tech space.

**Weight rule: regular (400) only. Bold (700) is forbidden.** The rounded quality of Corben at regular weight is what makes it feel like Waldo. Bold Corben is heavy and loses the warmth entirely.

Used for: hero headlines, section headlines, card headlines, the Nap Score number, all marketing headlines.

Never use for: body copy, labels, buttons, navigation, any UI element other than headlines and score display.

### DM Sans — everything else

Google Fonts. Three weights in use: **Regular (400)**, **Italic (400i)**, and **Medium (500)**. No other weights.

- Regular: all body copy, labels, captions, most UI text
- Italic: wit asides, *parenthetical commentary*, the human register (see section 11)
- Medium: button text, emphasis within body copy, active navigation labels

Never use: Bold (700), Light (300), or any weight not listed above. Never Inter. Never SF Pro. Never system-ui.

## Type scale

| Role | Font | Weight | Size | Line height |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hero headline | Corben | 400 | 52px | 1.08 |
| Section headline | Corben | 400 | 40px | 1.10 |
| Card headline | Corben | 400 | 28px | 1.20 |
| Nap Score number | Corben | 400 | 72px | 1.00 |
| Body | DM Sans | 400 | 16px | 1.70 |
| Body emphasis | DM Sans | 500 | 16px | 1.70 |
| Wit aside | DM Sans | 400i | 14px | 1.60 |
| Label | DM Sans | 400 | 13px | 1.50 |
| Badge / micro | DM Sans | 500 | 11px | 1.40 |
| Button | DM Sans | 500 | 15px | 1.00 |
| Source / app label | DM Sans | 500 | 11px | 1.40 |

## Type rules

**Sentence case always.** Never title case. Never all caps. Applies everywhere — headlines, buttons, labels, notifications, error messages. The only exception is "Waldo" as a proper noun.

**Headlines say one thing.** No subtitle immediately below a hero headline. The headline carries the idea. Body copy carries the explanation. Never crowd them.

**Regular Corben only.** If you find yourself reaching for bold Corben — stop. Use a smaller size or reconsider the hierarchy instead.

**Italic DM Sans is the wit register.** It is the sound of Waldo knowing something you don't, and choosing to mention it quietly. It is used exclusively for the human asides — the parenthetical commentary that makes Waldo feel like a person rather than a product. It is never used for functional emphasis.

# 09 · Layout and containers

## The grid

8px base grid. All spacing values are multiples of 8.
Common values: 8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96.

Responsive column grid:
- Desktop: 12 columns, 24px gutters, 80px outer margin
- Tablet: 8 columns, 20px gutters, 40px outer margin
- Mobile: 4 columns, 16px gutters, 24px outer margin

## Text blocks — the shape rule

This is a defining characteristic of Waldo's typographic identity. **Text blocks must form a deliberate shape — triangular or oval. Never a random ragged edge.**

**Triangular blocks:** Each line is shorter than the one above. The copy tapers toward a point. Used for hero headlines where the final word or phrase carries the emphasis.

```
Your health didn't
sign up for
any of this.
```

**Oval / lens blocks:** Lines build to a widest point in the middle and taper on both ends. Used for body copy, card text, and centred section copy where the block should feel complete and contained.

```
  Your watch has been collecting sleep,
HRV, recovery, and stress data every day.
  Not in a hospital. Not in a lab.
        On your wrist.
```

**The rule:** Before any copy goes to design, sketch the block shape. If the lines are random widths with no deliberate silhouette, rewrite the line breaks until the shape is intentional. A ragged, asymmetric text block is a mistake, not a style.

## Cards and containers

### Notification cards (used in hero section)

White surface `#FAFAF8`. Border `1px solid rgba(26,26,26,0.10)`. Radius `16px`. Padding `24px`. No shadow.

**Structure:**
```
Source label        ← DM Sans 500 11px, text secondary
Headline text       ← Corben 400 28px, text primary
*Wit aside*         ← DM Sans 400i 14px, text tertiary
```

The wit aside sits below the headline with a 4px gap. It is visually quieter — tertiary text colour, italic, smaller. It should feel like a whisper after a statement.

In dark mode: background `#242424`, border `rgba(250,250,248,0.08)`.

### Waldo message cards (Morning Wag, Adjustment, Spot)

White surface `#FAFAF8`. Border `1px solid rgba(26,26,26,0.10)`. Radius `16px`. Padding `28px 28px 24px`. No shadow.

**Structure:**
```
Paw print icon (24px)    ← top left
                         ← 16px gap
Body text (two paragraphs max)   ← DM Sans 400 16px, 1.7 line height
                         ← 12px gap
*Wit aside*              ← DM Sans 400i 14px, text tertiary
```

The paw print icon sits at top left at 24px. When the mascot illustration is present (larger card format), it replaces the paw print — never both.

In dark mode: background `#242424`.

### Feature / section cards

Used for The Daily Brief, The Patrol, The Adjustment, The Spot, The Constellation feature descriptions.

Full bleed within their section container. No card outline. The copy block is the container.

**Structure:**
```
Eyebrow    ← DM Sans 400i 13px, text tertiary. Italic. Lowercase.
Headline   ← Corben 400 40px, text primary
Body       ← DM Sans 400 16px, text secondary, max-width 560px centred
```

12px gap between eyebrow and headline. 24px gap between headline and body.

### Section backgrounds

| Section | Light mode | Dark mode |
|---|---|---|
| Default | `#FAFAF8` | `#1A1A1A` |
| Offset panel | `#F4F3F0` | `#242424` |
| Dark hero | `#1A1A1A` | `#1A1A1A` |
| Zone-tinted | Zone background colour | Zone dark background colour |

**The offset panel** — used for the health data section, feature details, and any section that needs visual separation without a hard colour change. It is a subtle step darker than the page background. Never a border, never a shadow — just the surface change.

## White space

White space is not empty space. It is part of the brand character. Waldo is restrained. Restrained means room to breathe.

Section padding: 96px top and bottom on desktop, 64px on mobile. Never less.

## Hierarchy rule

Every layout has one dominant idea. Not two. Not three. One. If a layout has two elements competing for dominance, remove one.

# 10 · Voice and language

## The character

Waldo sounds like a calm, trusted friend who happens to have a medical degree and has been paying close attention.

Not a doctor. Not a chatbot. Not a wellness coach. A friend who is already three steps ahead, and who says so without making a big deal of it.

## The formula

**Open warm → shift to quiet authority → close with understated confidence.**

Wit lands once, then stops. Every message.

## The Alfred register

Alfred from Batman is the correct reference. Warm, witty, quietly brilliant. Never self-congratulatory. Always already done it.

The dial: 40% Alfred, 60% friendly-first.

## Voice examples — locked

**Morning Wag:**
> "Morning. Bit of a rough night — your sleep was short by about 40 minutes. I've nudged your 9am to 10:30. Nothing drastic. The rest of your day looks good."

**Fetch Alert:**
> "Waldo did a thing. Your 2pm and 3pm swapped — the harder one's now when you're sharper. You can undo it if you want, but you probably don't."

**Pattern detected:**
> "Spotted something. Your Wednesdays are quietly your best day — consistently. I've been reserving your most demanding tasks for then. It appears to be working."

**Poor sleep:**
> "Last night was a difficult one. I've cleared your morning and pushed your first meeting to 10:30. I'd suggest a light start."

**Onboarding — screen 1:**
> "Hello. I'm Waldo. I'll be reading the health data your watch already collects — and quietly handling what needs handling. You won't hear from me unless there's something worth saying."

**Onboarding — permissions:**
> "Your health data, your calendar, your tasks. Nothing leaves your phone. I read it so I can act on it — that's the arrangement."

**Onboarding — final screen:**
> "Give me a few days. I need to learn your patterns before I start moving things around. After that, you'll notice the difference before you notice me."

## Personality zones — voice shifts with Nap Score

| Zone | Score | Voice register |
|---|---|---|
| Peak | 80–100 | Upbeat, challenge-oriented. Push toward deep work. A bit more wit permitted. |
| Steady | 60–79 | Warm, specific. One thing to watch. |
| Flagging | 40–59 | Honest, protective. One priority named. |
| Depleted | 0–39 | Minimal. One sentence. No pressure. |

## Words that are never used

`wellness`, `wellbeing`, `mindfulness`, `holistic`, `optimize`, `AI-powered`, `health tracker`, `unlock your potential`, `burnout` (as self-descriptor), `monitors`, `tracks`, `smart` (standalone), `intelligent` (standalone), `journey`, `empower`, `hustle`, `supercharge`, `game-changer`, `seamless`, `intuitive`, `powerful`, `revolutionary`

## Words Waldo owns

`biological intelligence`, `cognitive readiness`, `signal`, `before`, `pattern`, `baseline`, `capacity`, `reads`, `intervention`, `already knows`, `spotted`, `quiet`, `handled`, `arranged`

## Grammar rules

- Short sentences. If you can split it, split it.
- No exclamation marks. Not on positive states. Not on alerts.
- No questions in notifications. Waldo acts, doesn't ask.
- Numbers under ten are words in body copy. Data values are numerals.
- Time: 12-hour clock, am/pm lowercase. "10:30am."

# 11 · The human register — wit and sarcasm

This is new territory for Waldo and it is one of the most important things to get right.

## What it is

The wit register is the italic aside — the parenthetical commentary that Waldo (and the brand) uses to acknowledge the absurdity of the situation without breaking the professional register. It is the sound of a very competent person being quietly, drily funny about something that would otherwise just be depressing.

It is not snark. It is not cruelty. It is recognition. The user reads it and thinks: *this thing understands exactly what I'm dealing with.*

## Where it lives

**In notification cards (problem framing):**
The italic aside appears after the notification content to name the unspoken reality. It is written in second-person or as a self-aware observation. It is Waldo noticing, not judging.

```
Slack
Suyash, need to talk about the sales review for Q1...
*Sunday, 11:43pm, really?*

Slack
Hey, can I get the data for Q1 sales??? prep for the
meet tomorrow, wait no actually today...
*it's 3:58am. Interns just don't sleep.*

iMessage
"It was a great night, hope you did not watch the
next episodes without me"
*you binged the season on a Sunday night, bad call...*

Calendar
Hope you are ready for the 96th product strategy
review this week. See you at 9 on a Monday!
*here we go again...*
```

**In Waldo message cards (the resolution):**
After Waldo's briefing, the wit aside appears as a cultural reference or a quiet joke that signals Waldo's warmth. Not every card has one — only when the moment earns it.

```
Morning. Bit of a rough night — your sleep was short by about 90 minutes.
Nudged your 9am to 10:30 & 10am to noon. The rest of your day looks good.

*cues World Hold On by Bob Sinclair*
```

The cultural reference (the Bob Sinclair song) does something specific: it signals that Waldo has sorted everything and is now in a good mood about it. It is the equivalent of a competent surgeon humming while they work.

## The rules for wit asides

**Format:** Always in DM Sans italic 14px. Always in text tertiary colour. Always preceded by a blank line. Always lowercase. Always inside asterisks in copy documents — these translate to italic in the rendered product.

**Tone:** Observational, not mean. The aside names a truth the reader already feels. It never invents a problem or exaggerates. It simply says, quietly, "yes, this is exactly as ridiculous as you think it is."

**Frequency:** One per card, maximum. Often none. The aside earns its place. When every card has one, none of them land.

**Never:** An aside that judges the user. An aside that is funnier than the message it follows. An aside that requires explanation. If you have to explain why it's funny, it isn't.

## The problem/solution arc in two registers

The notification stack uses sarcastic asides to build empathy — the reader sees their own life reflected. Then the Waldo card resolves it with calm competence and a warm aside. This is the full emotional arc of the product compressed into a hero section:

**Recognition** (the problem cards with dry asides) →
**Relief** (the Waldo card that has already handled it) →
**Warmth** (the Bob Sinclair moment at the end)

That arc must be preserved whenever this pattern is used.

## Writing wit asides — the test

Before publishing any wit aside, ask: would a very smart, very calm, very competent person say this to a friend while solving their problem for them? If yes — it belongs. If it feels like a joke for its own sake — remove it.

# 13 · Illustration system

## Purpose

Illustration does work that copy cannot. It carries the emotional register of the product moment and makes the mascot feel present without speaking.

Every illustration should answer one question: what is Waldo already doing in this image? If the answer is nothing — the illustration is not earning its place.

## Style specification

**Line work:** Bold, confident outlines. Slight natural wobble. Chalk-like texture. Not digitally smoothed.

**Colour discipline:** Two brand colours maximum per illustration. Waldo Black for linework and fill. Waldo Orange as accent — once per illustration.

**Spots:** Solid filled irregular ovals. No outline around spots. Size variation within clusters. Slight rotation on individual spots.

**Background:** `#FAFAF8`. Never pure white. Never coloured.

**Fills:** Flat. No gradients. No shadows. No shading.

## The eight animation states

| State | Trigger | Where it appears |
|---|---|---|
| `watching` | Default, syncing, data loading | Daily Brief delivery, default chat, onboarding 1–2 |
| `good` | Nap Score 80+, positive morning | Dashboard at Peak |
| `good-sleep` | Sleep debt cleared, recovery complete | Sleep card positive state |
| `good-week` | 7-day positive trend | Weekly summary |
| `flagging` | Nap Score 40–59, Fetch Alert | Dashboard at Flagging |
| `rough-night` | Sleep < 5h, HRV significantly below baseline | Dashboard at Depleted |
| `thinking-working` | Waldo processing, loading | All loading states |
| `on-it` | Active Adjustment in progress | Adjustment card |

## Illustration rules

The mascot is never given a speech bubble. At full size: Dashboard top and onboarding only. Everywhere else: 24–48px paw print or avatar form.

**Dark mode:** Filled silhouette with soft warm glow offset — not stroke inversion. The illustration gains weight in dark mode.

# 14 · Animation

## Philosophy

Spring physics, not easing curves. Layered timing with delay cascade (eyes first, nose 60ms later, head 80ms after, ears 160ms after). Continuous idle breathing. Canvas rendering. Irregular hold timing.

State transitions are animated, never hard cuts. The `thinking-working` state has a distinctly slower tempo. Never use CSS spinners.

# 15 · Photography and imagery

Candid, unstaged moments. Genuine rest, genuine focus, genuine comfort. Not posed wellness photography.

**The test:** Does this person look like they had to think about looking this good? If yes — wrong image.

Lighting: warm, natural. No studio lighting. No filters. No blue-tinted images. No yoga. No green smoothies. No overhead flat-lays.

Wearables appear incidentally — on a wrist in a natural photo. Never as a hero product shot.

# 16 · Applying the brand — reserved to expressive

## Reserved

Black on white. Corben headline only. No illustration. Maximum white space. Contexts: legal, investor materials, error states, data-heavy views.

## Standard

Full colour palette in correct proportion. Both typefaces in use. Orange accent once per layout. Wit aside where earned. Contexts: app screens, social, email, landing page sections.

## Expressive

Large illustration. Large Corben regular. Zone colour or dark background. Orange accent used deliberately. Copy at its most confident. Contexts: hero section, App Store, major marketing moments, onboarding screen 1.

## The test

At any point: is this too much or too little? Too much — competing elements, multiple accents, illustration decorating rather than acting. Too little — generic type, no personality in copy, illustration absent when it would add warmth.

# 17 · Digital product standards

## The product is the brand

Every decision in the product — every state, every label, every transition — is a brand decision.

## Screen principles

**Dashboard:** Illustration leads. Nap Score follows. Daily Brief is the most important text element. Nothing competes with these three.

**Timeline:** Dots as Dalmatian spots. Constellations forming between high-score days.

**Constellation:** Always dark mode. Nodes in spot shape — irregular oval, not circle. The premium argument.

**Chat:** iMessage register, not chatbot. Waldo speaks first. No "AI Assistant" anywhere.

**Onboarding:** One screen at a time. No progress dots. No skip option. Illustration always visible.

## Push notifications

Format: "Waldo · [time]" as header. Two lines maximum. Never an exclamation mark. Never a question.

```
Waldo · 7:14am
78 today. Focus window 9–10:30. The API review goes first.

Waldo · Just now
Moved your 2pm. The harder one's now when you're sharper.
```

Push notifications do not use wit asides — they are too brief and too interruptive. The wit register is reserved for cards that have room to breathe.

## Empty states

```
"I'm still learning your patterns. Check back in a day or two."
"Nothing to flag. That's the idea."
"You're all caught up. I'll let you know if that changes."
```

Never: "No data yet 😊" · "Wow, looking great!" · "Nothing here yet — let's get started!"

## Error states

```
"Something didn't connect. Worth trying again in a moment."
"I lost the thread there. Give me a second."
```

Never: "Oops!" · "Something went wrong!" · "Uh oh!"

# 18 · Print and marketing applications

## Landing page — heywaldo.ai

**Hero:** Notification stack, no headline above it. The notifications land cold. After the stack: one pivot line — "Your health didn't sign up for any of this." in Corben 40px, centred.

**Health data section:** Offset panel background `#F4F3F0`. Eyebrow italic: "You already have everything Waldo needs." Headline: "Months of data. Nobody using it." Apple Health device imagery below the body copy.

**Solution pivot:** Waldo card centred. Headline above: "This is what you wake up to instead." No subhead. The card says everything.

**Feature sections:** Each feature has its eyebrow italic, Corben headline, DM Sans body. Text blocks in triangular or oval shapes. Never random line breaks.

**Closing CTA:** "100 years is a long time. Waldo makes sure you're ready for all of them." Centred. Large Corben regular. Microcopy: "No credit card. Cancel whenever. You probably won't want to."

## App Store

Screenshots tell the story in sequence: problem → data → solution → proof. Waldo illustration in at least two of five screenshots. Description maximum 170 words. No feature list. First sentence carries everything.

## Social

Minimal copy. One thought, one line. The mascot never speaks. Never a speech bubble.

## Email

Marketing: one illustration, one headline, one button. Wit aside only if the email has a moment that earns it. Transactional: plain text preferred.

# 19 · What Waldo is not

**Waldo is not a health tracker.** It does not show charts of your sleep. It does not give you a score and leave you to figure out what to do with it.

**Waldo is not a productivity tool.** Task management is an input to Waldo, not a product category it occupies.

**Waldo is not a wellness brand.** No green smoothies. No meditation prompts. No "how are you feeling today?"

**Waldo is not a chatbot.** You do not have to talk to Waldo for Waldo to work. Waldo talks to you when something is worth saying.

**Waldo is not gamified.** No streaks. No badges. No "You've completed 7 days in a row!"

**Waldo is not anxious.** It does not alert you constantly. It does not create urgency where none exists.

**Waldo is not apologetic.** "You can undo it if you want, but you probably don't." That is the register.

**Waldo is not an AI brand.** It does not describe itself as AI-powered, intelligent, or smart. It is Waldo. That is enough.
