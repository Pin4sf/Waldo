## Part I: The Universal Human Truth

Before demographics, before features, before positioning — there's one truth that makes Waldo relevant to every human being on Earth:

> **Your body is running a calculation you can't see, and it determines the outcome of everything you do.**

The job interview you bombed? Your HRV was 40% below baseline that morning from accumulated sleep debt. The brilliant idea you had in the shower? Your circadian rhythm peaked and your parasympathetic nervous system was dominant. The argument with your partner last Tuesday? Your cortisol had been elevated since a stressful meeting at 3pm and you hadn't recovered.

**Every human makes decisions with a body they don't understand.** No technology today bridges this gap. Health apps show numbers. Productivity apps push tasks. Neither connects the two.

Waldo is the first thing that reads your body AND acts on what it finds — before you even notice.

That's the universal pitch. Now let's break down what this means for every person alive.

## Part II: The Seven Domains of Life Waldo Touches

Not organized by technical phase. Organized by how a human experiences their day.

### Domain 1: How You Start Your Day (Morning Intelligence)

**What Waldo does:**
- Reads your overnight biology (sleep quality, HRV recovery, sleep debt, circadian alignment)
- Checks what's ahead (calendar, tasks due, communication backlog)
- Delivers ONE message before you've finished your coffee: "Here's how your body prepared for today, and here's what I've already adjusted"

**What this looks like in practice:**
- "6.5h sleep, deep sleep was solid. CRS 74 — steady day ahead. Your standup is at 9, but your peak window opens at 10. Draft that proposal then, not now."
- "Rough night. CRS 38. I moved your non-critical 10am to Thursday. Only one thing today: the client call at 2pm. I'll prep you 30 min before."
- "You slept 9 hours and your HRV bounced 20% above baseline. Whatever you did yesterday worked. Big energy today — don't waste it on email."

**Who cares about this:**
- Literally everyone. Every human wakes up and faces their day. The Morning Wag is the universal entry point.

### Domain 2: How You Spend Your Energy (Work & Productivity Intelligence)

**What Waldo does:**
- Matches task difficulty to cognitive state
- Protects deep work windows from interruption
- Sequences your day: hardest at peak, admin during dips, nothing during depleted
- Breaks overwhelming tasks into biology-sized chunks
- Tells you when to push and when to stop — not based on time, based on your body

**What this looks like:**
- "3 tasks due today. Your CRS peaks at 10:30. I'd tackle the architecture doc then. Save Slack catchup for the 2pm dip."
- "You've been above CRS 80 for 3 hours. Rare day. Whatever you've been avoiding — now is the time."
- "CRS 42, 3h sleep debt, 5 tasks overdue. Here's what I'd do: pick the ONE that unblocks everyone else. The rest can wait 24 hours. Your predicted CRS tomorrow is 68."
- "You've been in flow for 90 minutes and your HRV is stable. I'm blocking your calendar and setting DND. Keep going."

**Who cares about this:**
- Knowledge workers, founders, engineers, students, creatives — anyone whose output depends on cognitive quality

### Domain 3: How You Handle Stress (Emotional & Physiological Regulation)

**What Waldo does:**
- Detects stress from biometric signals BEFORE you consciously register it
- Intervenes with micro-actions (2-min breathing, step outside, water)
- Tracks which interventions actually work FOR YOU over time
- Learns your stress triggers (specific meetings, specific people, specific times)
- Over months, predicts stress before it happens based on schedule + biology patterns

**What this looks like:**
- "Your HRV dropped 25% over the last 15 minutes while you were sedentary. Something's spiking. Take 2 minutes. Nothing else matters right now."
- "I've noticed your stress confidence hits 0.7+ every Tuesday between 2-4pm. That's your recurring 1:1 with [person]. Want preparation strategies?"
- "Breathing exercises work 72% of the time for you. Walk breaks only 45%. I'll suggest breathing first next time."
- "Three high-stress days in a row. Your 7-day HRV baseline is trending down. This is the beginning of a pattern. Let's protect tomorrow."

**Who cares about this:**
- Everyone. But especially: people with anxiety, high-pressure workers, parents, caregivers, anyone who says "I'm fine" when they're not.

### Domain 4: How You Sleep (Recovery Intelligence)

**What Waldo does:**
- Active sleep coaching — not just tracking, but intervening
- Bedtime nudges timed to YOUR circadian rhythm (not a generic "10pm" alarm)
- Screen time reduction prompts before bed
- Caffeine timing ("No coffee after 2pm for you — your metabolism clears it slowly")
- Sleep debt management — knows when you need to bank extra hours before a big week
- Pre-travel sleep prep for jet lag management

**What this looks like:**
- "You're 2 hours from your optimal bedtime. Start winding down. Your deep sleep drops 30% when you go to bed after midnight."
- "Sleep debt: 4.2 hours accumulated this week. Tomorrow's calendar is light. Protect that time — your body needs it."
- "You fly to New York Thursday. Based on your chronotype, I'd suggest shifting bedtime 30 min earlier each night starting tonight."
- "Your deep sleep has improved 15% since you started the magnesium. Pattern confirmed over 3 weeks."

**Who cares about this:**
- Insomniacs, parents of newborns, shift workers, frequent travelers, students during exam season, literally anyone who sleeps

### Domain 5: How You Connect With Others (Social & Relationship Intelligence)

**What Waldo does:**

**Who cares about this:**

### Domain 6: How You Move & Recover (Physical Intelligence)

**What Waldo does:**

**Who cares about this:**
- Athletes, gym-goers, runners, fitness enthusiasts, anyone with a watch who exercises

### Domain 7: How You Make Decisions (Cognitive Quality Intelligence)

This is the **highest-value domain** and the one no other product touches.

**What Waldo does:**
- Times high-stakes decisions to peak cognitive windows
- Warns against critical decisions during depleted states
- Tracks decision quality correlation with biological state over months
- Learns YOUR patterns: "Your best presentations happen at CRS 75+ after 7h sleep"
- Pre-decision state check: "You're about to send this investor email. CRS is 43. Want to draft it now and send tomorrow at 10am when you're typically at 78?"

**What this looks like:**
- "You have a hiring decision to make. Your CRS has been below 50 for 3 days. If it's not time-critical, sit on it until Thursday."
- "Financial decisions made during your depleted windows cost you on average. Last 3 impulse purchases were all on CRS-below-45 days."
- "Your negotiation is at 2pm. Your CRS is projected to dip at 1:30 (post-lunch circadian trough). Eat light. Walk for 10 min at 1:15."
- "You've been coding for 4 hours. Your HRV just started declining. Commit what you have. Review it tomorrow fresh."

**Who cares about this:**
- Founders, investors, executives, traders, lawyers, doctors, pilots, anyone making consequential decisions

## Part III: What Waldo Means to Every Person

### The Student

**Pain points:** Study efficiency, sleep deprivation, exam anxiety, all-nighter culture, ADHD/executive function struggles, social pressure vs academic demands

**What Waldo does:**
- **Study-sleep optimizer:** "Your brain retains 40% more after 7h sleep than after an all-nighter. You have 3 days until your exam. Here's a study schedule that maximizes retention while protecting sleep."
- **Exam prep:** Build sleep credit the week before. Know your peak study windows. Time revision to circadian peaks.
- **ADHD support:** Executive function is directly impacted by sleep and HRV. Waldo tracks this correlation. "Your focus ability today is lower than usual. Start with the easiest task to build momentum. Switch to the hard one in 90 minutes when your circadian peaks."
- **Social balance:** "You're depleted. Skip the party tonight — you'll enjoy it more when you're rested. Saturday looks clear."
- **Pomodoro on steroids:** Not arbitrary 25-min blocks. Biology-timed blocks based on actual cognitive stamina.

**The "aha" moment:** "It told me to stop studying and sleep at 11pm. I trusted it. Got my best exam score."

### The Software Engineer

**Pain points:** Deep work disruption, meeting fatigue, on-call burnout, PR review quality when tired, imposter syndrome amplified by exhaustion

**What Waldo does:**
- **Deep work guardian:** Detects and protects peak cognitive windows. Auto-blocks calendar. Sets DND.
- **PR review timing:** "Don't review this complex PR right now. Your CRS is 43. You'll miss bugs. Queue it for tomorrow morning."
- **On-call recovery:** After a 2am pager alert, Waldo adjusts the next day: lighter schedule, delayed standup, recovery mode.
- **Burnout trajectory:** "Your 30-day HRV trend is declining. You've worked 11h+ for 8 of the last 14 days. This is a burnout trajectory."
- **Commit quality correlation:** Over time, learns that your code quality drops below CRS 50.

**The "aha" moment:** "It caught me about to push a PR at midnight with CRS 35. I waited until 10am. Found two bugs I'd missed."

### The Startup Founder

**Pain points:** No boundaries, decision fatigue, investor meetings while exhausted, team management while depleted, the myth of hustle culture

**What Waldo does:**
- **Decision timing:** Times investor calls, hiring decisions, strategic planning to peak CRS windows

- **Meeting triage:** "You have 7 meetings today. Based on your CRS (62) and the meeting types, here's the order: move the investor sync to 10am, put the 1:1s after lunch, cancel the optional standup."

- **Co-founder alignment (Pack tier):** Both founders see each other's readiness. "Neither of you should be making hiring decisions today."

**The "aha" moment:** "It told me my CRS was 39 before an investor call and suggested moving it to Thursday. Thursday I was at 81. We got the term sheet."

**The "aha" moment:** "It told both of us we were depleted and to order pizza and not talk about anything serious. Saved our marriage that night."
